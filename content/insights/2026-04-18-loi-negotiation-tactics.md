Letter of intent negotiation establishes deal terms before extensive investment. Buyers who negotiate effectively achieve better economics. This memo analyzes LOI negotiation tactics.

## The LOI Function

Letter of intent serves specific functions in acquisition process.

+ Establishes key terms before detailed due diligence.
+ Creates framework for definitive agreement negotiation.
+ Signals serious interest to seller.
+ Manages expectations for both parties.

LOI is not binding but creates expectations.

## Key Terms to Address

Specific terms require LOI attention.

+ Purchase price: enterprise value and structure.
+ Transaction structure: asset versus stock.
+ Financing: buyer financing obligations.
+ Due diligence: scope and timing.
+ Closing conditions: conditions to closing.
+ Employee matters: key employee retention.
+ Representations: seller representations scope.

Comprehensive LOI reduces later disputes.

## Binding Versus Non-Binding

LOI binding provisions require consideration.

+ Binding: price, exclusivity, confidentiality often binding.
+ Non-binding: other terms serve as framework.
+ Hybrid approach: specific provisions binding, others not.

Structure should match deal circumstances.

## Exclusivity Provisions

Exclusivity creates commitment from seller.

+ Duration: typically 30-90 days.
+ Scope: prevents competing offers.
+ Extensions: possible extension by mutual agreement.
+ Break-up fees: may be included.

Exclusivity enables serious negotiation.

## Price Mechanisms

LOI should address price structure.

+ Fixed price:确定的 price.
+ Adjustment mechanism: working capital, other adjustments.
+ Earnout: contingent payments.
+ Escrow: holdback for post-closing issues.

Price mechanism affects risk allocation.

## Contingencies

LOI contingencies protect buyer interests.

+ Financing contingency: buyer obligation subject to financing.
+ Due diligence contingency: buyer satisfaction with due diligence.
+ Third-party consent: required approvals.
+ Material adverse change: protection against deterioration.

Contingencies enable exit if issues emerge.

## Timeline Provisions

LOI should establish timeline expectations.

+ Due diligence period: time for buyer investigation.
+ Definitive agreement: time for documentation.
+ Closing: target closing date.
+ Extensions: provisions for timeline extension.

Timeline clarity manages expectations.

## Negotiation Approach

LOI negotiation requires specific approach.

+ Establish walk-away points before negotiation.
+ Focus on substance over positioning.
+ Build relationship for later negotiation.
+ Maintain flexibility for changing circumstances.

Effective negotiation preserves deal economics.
