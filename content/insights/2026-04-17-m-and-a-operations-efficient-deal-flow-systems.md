## Process Architecture Design

Process architecture defines systematic approaches to deal flow activities. Architecture must balance standardization against appropriate variation. Over-standardization prevents adaptation that market changes require. Under-standardization creates inconsistency that undermines quality across transactions.

Stage-gate processes structure deal progression through defined phases with specific requirements. Qualification gates ensure appropriate advancement criteria prevent premature investment in poor candidates. The stage-gate approach maintains pipeline quality as opportunities progress through evaluation. Organizations that implement stage-gate discipline reduce wasted effort on opportunities that fail basic screening.

Checklist discipline ensures consistent coverage across transactions that memory alone cannot provide. Due diligence checklists prevent missed items that create post-close problems. Closing checklists prevent delays from forgotten requirements. Integration checklists ensure comprehensive planning. The checklist approach standardizes execution while allowing appropriate variation for deal-specific needs. Checklists represent accumulated wisdom from past transactions.

Template libraries accelerate document preparation that would otherwise consume extensive analyst time. NDAs, LOIs, and integration plans start from standardized foundations that ensure comprehensive coverage. The template approach improves quality while reducing preparation time across all transactions. Templates encode best practices that junior team members might otherwise miss.

Escalation protocols ensure appropriate guidance on complex issues that front-line teams cannot resolve alone. Defined escalation criteria prevent both under-reaction and over-reaction. Routing procedures ensure escalation reaches qualified decision-makers. Resolution authorities enable decisive action without committee approval. The protocol approach ensures problems receive appropriate attention from those with authority to resolve them.
