Sellers are not rational actors. Psychology drives decisions that logic does not explain. Understanding seller psychology improves conversion.

## The Emotional Drivers

Sale decisions have emotional components.

> Fear drives urgency. Owners fear declining values, health issues, market changes.
> Pride drives legacy. Owners want businesses to continue thriving.
> Fatigue drives exit. Owners burnout and want to move on.
> Greed drives maximization. Owners want maximum value.

The emotional drivers vary by owner and situation.

## The Trust Factors

Sellers trust buyers based on specific factors.

* Demonstrated capability to close.
* History of fair dealing.
* Respect for employees and business.
* Clear communication and process.

The trust factors determine whether sellers engage.

## The Decision Dynamics

Seller decisions follow patterns.

+ Initial resistance often means not ready.
+ Repeated contact may convert resistance.
* Decision often comes at predictable moments.
* External events often trigger decisions.

The patterns enable better engagement.

## The Communication Approach

Communication should address psychology.

* Acknowledge emotional complexity.
* Build trust before pushing for decision.
* Time communication with decision triggers.
* Respect the difficulty of letting go.

The approach improves conversion.
