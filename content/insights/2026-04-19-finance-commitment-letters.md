Financing commitment letters establish capital availability before closing. Buyers with committed capital achieve better deal terms. This memo analyzes financing commitment letters.

## Commitment Letter Function

Commitment letters establish lender obligations.

+ Demonstrates buyer financing capability.
+ Creates timeline for financing completion.
+ Establishes terms for financing.
+ Protects buyer deposit.

Commitment letters facilitate deal execution.

## Commitment Types

Multiple commitment types exist.

+ Highly confident letter: informal indication of capability.
+ Conditional commitment: commitment subject to conditions.
+ Absolute commitment: firm obligation to fund.

Commitment type affects seller confidence.

## Conditions Precedent

Commitment letters include specific conditions.

+ Due diligence satisfactory to lender.
+ No material adverse change.
+ Third-party consents obtained.
+ Documentation satisfactory to lender.

Conditions should be manageable.

## Terms and Pricing

Commitment letters establish terms.

+ Interest rate: market rate plus spread.
+ Fees: commitment fees, facility fees.
+ Covenants: financial covenants required.
+ Collateral: security requirements.

Terms should be acceptable.

## Timeline Management

Commitment letters include timeline requirements.

+ Commitment period: time to close financing.
+ Funding conditions: conditions to funding.
+ Extension provisions: possible extension.

Timeline should align with transaction timeline.

## Fallback Provisions

Financing fallback provides alternatives.

+ Multiple financing sources: backup options.
+ Flexible terms: alternative structures.
+ Bridge financing: interim financing.

Fallback provisions provide security.

## Commitment Letter Negotiation

Negotiation affects commitment terms.

+ Commitment strength: more committed letters require better terms.
+ Conditions: negotiate manageable conditions.
+ Fees: commitment fees affect economics.
+ Timeline: negotiate appropriate periods.

Negotiation affects deal flexibility.

## Sammanfattning

Financing commitment letters establish capital availability. Multiple commitment types exist. Conditions should be manageable. Terms should be acceptable. Timeline should align with transaction. Fallback provisions provide security. Negotiation affects commitment strength.
