Key person dependence creates significant business risk. Buyers who understand and address key person dependence achieve better outcomes. This memo analyzes key person dependence.

## The Dependence Problem

Key person dependence exists when business value depends significantly on specific individuals.

+ Owner dependence: business value resides in owner's knowledge and relationships.
+ Key employee dependence: critical personnel with specialized skills.
+ Customer relationship dependence: relationships held by specific individuals.
+ Technical dependence: unique technical knowledge held by few people.

Dependence creates risk that manifests post-acquisition.

## Dependence Assessment

Key person dependence assessment requires specific analysis.

+ Identify critical individuals: who would significantly affect performance if departed.
+ Assess knowledge concentration: where does unique knowledge reside.
+ Evaluate relationship depth: which relationships are person-dependent.
+ Analyze replacement difficulty: how difficult would replacement be.

Assessment should identify specific risks for mitigation planning.

## Valuation Implications

Key person dependence affects business valuation.

+ High dependence reduces valuation multiples.
+ Mitigation potential affects valuation impact.
+ Insurance availability affects risk transfer options.
+ Transition plans affect buyer confidence.

Valuation should reflect key person risk appropriately.

## Due Diligence Requirements

Key person due diligence requires specific analysis.

+ Employment agreements: terms, restrictions, enforceability.
+ Non-compete enforceability: geographic and temporal scope.
+ Historical retention: turnover rates, departure patterns.
+ Succession planning: what happens if key person departs.

Due diligence should assess both current state and transition risk.

## Retention Strategies

Key person retention requires specific strategies.

+ Employment agreements: secure terms for required retention period.
+ Compensation alignment: equity, bonuses tied to tenure.
+ Cultural fit: ensure alignment with new ownership.
+ Career development: growth opportunities under new ownership.

Retention strategies should match individual motivations.

## Transition Planning

Post-acquisition transition requires specific planning.

+ Relationship transfer: systematically transfer relationships to company.
+ Knowledge documentation: capture tacit knowledge.
+ Redundancy building: develop backup capabilities.
+ Customer introduction: facilitate customer relationships with new team.

Transition planning should assume key person departure.

## Insurance Solutions

Key person insurance provides risk transfer.

+ Life insurance: for owner death or disability.
+ Disability insurance: for key person incapacity.
+ Business interruption: for extended absence.
+ Buy-sell provisions: for ownership transition.

Insurance should be part of comprehensive key person risk management.
