The window of opportunity between when an owner decides to sell and when the business hits the market is narrow. Most buyers never see this window. The firms that capture it access deals without competition.

## The Decision Window

Business owners do not wake up one day and list their business for sale. The decision process unfolds over months or years. The window between decision and public market is when deals are cleanest.

* The owner internally decides that exit is preferable to continuation.
* The owner begins evaluating options.
* The owner contacts advisors about process.
* The owner engages a broker.
* The business hits the market.

The first four stages are invisible to general market participants. Only the firm with relationships or signals sees the opportunity. These deals have no competition. The terms reflect bilateral negotiation rather than auction dynamics.

## The Trigger Mapping

Owner decisions follow predictable patterns. Trigger mapping identifies when decisions are likely.

> Health issues create urgent timeline.
> Retirement age creates planning horizon.
> Partnership disputes create forced exits.
> Market consolidation creates strategic urgency.
> Burnout creates desire for change.

Each trigger has associated timelines. Health issues may require action within months. Retirement planning may span years. The mapping predicts decision timing.

* Combine trigger identification with timeline mapping.
* Predict when owners will move from contemplation to action.
* Engage at the moment of decision, not after.

## The Urgency Capture

When owners decide to sell, they want to complete quickly. The firms that can close faster capture better terms.

> Speed demonstrates capability.
> Speed reduces owner anxiety.
> Speed minimizes market exposure.
> Speed prevents competitive entry.

The firms with pre-built capabilities close faster. Pre-qualified financing. Ready due diligence teams. Established legal relationships. Accelerated processes.

+ Build closing infrastructure before needing it.
+ Pre-qualify for multiple financing structures.
+ Maintain relationships with legal and financial advisors.
+ Create fast-track processes that accelerate without compromising risk.

## The Patience Strategy

Capturing the decision moment requires patience. The firms that burn through opportunities quickly miss the ones that take time to develop.

* Some opportunities require years of relationship building.
* Some triggers require monitoring over extended periods.
* Some sellers require multiple touchpoints before engaging.

The patience strategy accepts that not every signal converts immediately. The follow-up system maintains relationships over time. The pipeline management tracks long-cycle opportunities.

## The First-Mover Advantage

First contact with a seller who has decided to sell creates enormous advantage. The relationship starts before competition exists.

* The first buyer establishes credibility.
* The first buyer shapes expectations.
* The first buyer controls the process.
* The first buyer often wins on terms.

The firms that optimize for first contact capture disproportionate value. The firms that optimize for activity volume miss the dynamics that matter.

The timing arbitrage is the ultimate origination advantage. Capture it or compete for crumbs.
