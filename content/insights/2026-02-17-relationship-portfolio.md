Deal flow comes from relationship portfolios. Managing portfolios affects results. The firms that manage portfolios systematically outperform those that manage randomly.

## The Portfolio Components

Relationship portfolios have multiple components.

> Advisor relationships: CPAs, attorneys, bankers, insurers.
> Industry relationships: Executives, consultants, trade associations.
> Founder relationships: Prior sellers, prospective sellers, referral sources.
> Platform relationships: Portfolio companies, portfolio executives.

The components vary by value and maintenance cost.

## The Portfolio Analysis

Analysis reveals portfolio health.

* Relationship value by category.
* Activity levels by relationship type.
* Deal flow from each category.
* Return on relationship investment.

The analysis informs management decisions.

## The Portfolio Optimization

Optimization improves portfolio performance.

+ Invest more in high-value relationships.
+ Maintain adequate coverage in all categories.
+ Develop new relationships strategically.
+ Prune non-performing relationships.

The optimization maximizes portfolio value.

## The Rebalancing Strategy

Periodic rebalancing maintains portfolio health.

* Annual review assesses relationship performance.
* New relationships fill gaps.
* Old relationships receive continued investment.
* Portfolio reflects current strategy.

The strategy maintains alignment.
