## The Sourcing Technology

Deal sourcing technology requires comprehensive infrastructure. The infrastructure divides into signal technology, processing technology, and engagement technology that collectively enable systematic target identification.

Signal technology monitors market indicators continuously. Automated surveillance identifies motivated sellers before broker involvement. The monitoring generates proprietary opportunities that random research cannot find. Implementation monitors four signal categories: ownership transitions (director's changes exceeding 20%, new beneficial owners, family succession triggers), financial distress (payment defaults, county court filings, tax liens, enforcement actions), lifecycle events (retirement announcements, health indicators, business dissolution filings), and regulatory changes (licensing modifications, compliance violations, sector investigations). The system processes 47,000 daily events generating 180+ qualified targets monthly without broker dependency. Proprietary identification before broker involvement creates 15-25% pricing advantage representing SEK 12-25 million per average transaction.

The signal technology applies GTM Engineering principles. Scrapy-based crawlers extract data from 847 source systems including Swedish court registers (tingsrätter), the Swedish Companies Registration Office (Bolagsverket), the Swedish Tax Agency (Skatteverket), and municipal business registries. Each source requires unique authentication handling: OAuth 2.0 for Skatteverket APIs, session cookies for Bolagsverket's JavaScript-heavy interface, and CAPTCHA solving for Kronofogden's enforcement database. The multi-source approach captures signals that single-source approaches miss, identifying 340% more distressed opportunities than broker networks alone. Machine learning classifiers trained on 89,000 historical transactions predict seller motivation with 78% accuracy, enabling prioritization of high-probability sellers for immediate outreach. The signal-to-outreach latency of 4.7 seconds creates decisive advantages in competitive situations.

Processing technology transforms signals efficiently. Firmographic enrichment, contact appending, and financial analysis execute automatically. The processing generates actionable intelligence without manual research. The transformation pipeline includes firmographic enrichment (company size, industry classification, location, subsidiary identification), contact appending (decision-maker names, verified email addresses, direct dial phone numbers), financial analysis (3-year revenue trends, EBITDA margins, debt levels, credit scores), and fit scoring (size alignment, sector fit, geography match, strategic fit probability). Processing velocity averages 4.2 minutes per target versus 45-90 minutes for manual research. Monthly capacity reaches 500+ targets without additional analyst headcount. The efficiency reduces cost per qualified opportunity from SEK 45,000 to SEK 8,200 while improving qualification consistency by 12%.

The processing technology implements GTM Engineering for autonomous transformation. Apache Airflow orchestrates 340 daily enrichment tasks across 12 worker nodes, each executing concurrent API calls to Bisnode, UC, Creditsafe, and Proff. Redis caching reduces redundant API calls by 67%, cutting enrichment costs from SEK 340 per target to SEK 112. Natural language processing models extract strategic signals from annual reports, identifying growth investments, workforce expansions, and succession planning that indicate potential sell-side motivation. The system maintains data freshness at 98.7% for firmographics, 94.2% for financials, and 89.3% for contacts. Contact verification employs email pattern matching against 12 million Swedish corporate addresses, achieving 87% deliverability versus 34% for manual approaches. Each enriched target outputs standardized JSON enabling immediate downstream engagement triggering.

The execution generates returns that random contact cannot achieve. LinkedIn sequences include connection requests, content engagement, and personalized InMail. Calling orchestration includes voicemail drops, call scheduling, and conversation logging. CRM integration tracks activities, stages, and outcomes with automated workflow triggers. Systematic execution generates 23% response rates versus 4% for random outreach.
