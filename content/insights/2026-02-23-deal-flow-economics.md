Deal flow has real costs. Understanding true costs enables optimization. The firms that calculate accurately make better decisions.

## The Cost Components

Deal flow costs have multiple components.

> Personnel: Salaries and bonuses for sourcing teams.
> Technology: CRM, data, and tooling costs.
> Marketing: Events, content, and presence expenses.
* Travel: In-person meeting and relationship costs.
* Time: Opportunity cost of senior leadership.

The components sum to total cost.

## The Return Components

Deal flow returns have multiple components.

* Acquired businesses generate returns.
* Relationships generate future deals.
* Reputation generates inbound.
* Knowledge generates insight.

The components vary in timing and certainty.

## The Calculation Framework

Deal flow ROI requires systematic calculation.

+ Track costs by category and activity.
+ Track returns by source and timing.
+ Calculate ROI by channel and strategy.
+ Optimize allocation based on ROI.

The framework reveals true economics.

## The Optimization Strategy

Optimization improves deal flow economics.

* Increase investment in high-ROI channels.
* Reduce investment in low-ROI channels.
* Test new channels for expansion.
* Terminate unprofitable activities.

The strategy maximizes return on investment.
