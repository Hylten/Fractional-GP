Technology transforms deal flow. New capabilities enable new approaches. The firms that adopt technology systematically outperform those that resist change.

## The Technology Categories

Deal flow technology spans multiple categories.

> Data technology: Information collection, processing, and analysis.
> Communication technology: Outreach, relationship management, engagement.
> Automation technology: Process execution, follow-up, tracking.
> Intelligence technology: Pattern recognition, prediction, optimization.

The categories cover the deal flow lifecycle.

## The Adoption Framework

Technology adoption requires systematic approach.

* Evaluate technology capabilities against needs.
* Pilot with limited scope.
* Scale successful implementations.
* Retire unsuccessful experiments.

The framework reduces adoption risk.

## The Integration Requirements

Technology requires integration to deliver value.

+ Connect systems for data flow.
+ Train teams on usage.
+ Align processes with capabilities.
* Measure impact on performance.

The requirements enable value capture.

## The Competitive Implications

Technology creates competitive implications.

* Early adopters gain advantage.
* Late adopters fall behind.
* Technology gaps create vulnerabilities.
* Integration creates moats.

The implications require strategic attention.
