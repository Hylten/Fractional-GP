Deal sourcing that relies on intuition and relationships misses opportunities that data can identify. The firms that build data infrastructure systematize insight that competitors cannot replicate.

## The Data Layers

Deal flow data infrastructure requires multiple integrated layers.

> The first layer is target data. Comprehensive databases of businesses meeting acquisition criteria. Coverage determines opportunity visibility. Quality determines targeting precision.

> The second layer is signal data. Information indicating potential seller motivation. Signals may be explicit or implicit. Signal collection requires systematic monitoring.

> The third layer is contextual data. Market conditions, sector dynamics, competitive activity. Context transforms data into intelligence.

> The fourth layer is outcome data. Historical conversion patterns, deal economics, success factors. Outcome data enables optimization.

The integrated layers create intelligence that drives decision-making.

## The Target Data Foundation

Target data requires comprehensive coverage and accurate classification.

* Revenue and EBITDA ranges define addressable markets.
* Geographic focus determines coverage requirements.
* Sector classification enables targeted outreach.
* Ownership data reveals potential sellers.

The target data should support precise filtering. The more precise the targeting, the more personalized the outreach. Generic lists produce generic results.

## The Signal Collection System

Signal data requires systematic collection from multiple sources.

> Industry publications reveal strategic shifts.
> Regulatory filings indicate sector changes.
> Professional network activity signals owner intentions.
> Business filings show ownership transitions.
> News sources capture market developments.

The signal collection system must filter noise from signal. Most information is irrelevant. The system identifies the relevant signals.

+ Automated monitoring for broad coverage.
+ Manual curation for quality control.
+ Prioritization algorithms for velocity.
+ Routing systems for response time.

## The Intelligence Synthesis

Data without analysis is cost. Intelligence requires synthesis.

* Signal patterns reveal motivation trends.
* Target behavior predicts conversion probability.
* Context factors influence timing.
* Outcome analysis informs approach optimization.

The synthesis transforms raw data into actionable intelligence. The intelligence drives decisions about where to focus resources.

## The Optimization Loop

Data infrastructure enables continuous optimization.

> What targets convert at highest rates?
> What signals predict seller motivation?
> What approaches generate responses?
> What timing optimizes conversion?

The optimization loop uses data to improve performance continuously. The firms that optimize systematically outperform those that rely on intuition.
