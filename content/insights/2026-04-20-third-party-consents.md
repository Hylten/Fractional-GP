Third-party consents affect transaction closing. Buyers who manage consent requirements achieve timely closings. This memo analyzes third-party consent management.

## Consent Categories

Multiple consent categories affect acquisitions.

+ Landlord consents: lease assignments.
+ Customer consents: contract assignments.
+ Supplier consents: supply agreement assignments.
+ Employee consents: employment agreement assignments.
+ Licensor consents: license agreements.
+ Regulatory consents: government approvals.

Categories require specific management.

## Identification Requirements

Consent identification should be early.

+ Review all material contracts.
+ Identify consent requirements.
+ Assess consent difficulty.
+ Build timeline accordingly.

Early identification enables planning.

## Consent Difficulty Assessment

Consents vary in difficulty.

+ Routine consents: typically obtainable.
+ Difficult consents: may require negotiation.
+ Hostile parties: refuse consent.
+ Silent parties: no response to requests.

Assessment informs strategy.

## Consent Strategy

Consent strategy should be specific.

+ Early engagement: approach consent parties early.
+ Relationship building: build support.
+ Documentation: prepare complete documentation.
+ Negotiation: negotiate acceptable terms.

Strategy should match difficulty.

## Consent Timing

Timing affects consent success.

+ Pre-closing consents: obtain before closing.
+ Post-closing consents: obtain after closing.
+ Contingency plans: plans if consents unavailable.

Timing should align with deal structure.

## Risk Allocation

Consent risk affects deal structure.

+ Consent failure: closing condition or contingency.
+ Liability allocation: who bears consent failure risk.
+ Closing impact: can close without consent.

Allocation affects transaction structure.

## Consent Documentation

Consent documentation should be clear.

+ Assignment instruments: proper assignment forms.
+ Estoppel certificates: confirmation of terms.
+ Amendment agreements: consent to changes.

Documentation should be complete.

## Sammanfattning

Third-party consents affect closing. Early identification enables planning. Difficulty varies by consent party. Strategy should match difficulty. Timing affects success. Risk allocation affects structure. Documentation should be complete.
