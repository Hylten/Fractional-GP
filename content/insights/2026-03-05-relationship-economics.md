Relationship investment generates return. Calculating the return enables optimization. The firms that measure relationship economics outperform those that guess.

## The Investment Components

Relationship investment has multiple components.

> Time investment: Hours spent building relationships.
> Resource investment: Capital spent on events and outreach.
> Opportunity investment: Foregone deals from relationship constraints.
> Personnel investment: Staff dedicated to relationship building.

The components sum to total relationship investment.

## The Return Components

Relationship return manifests in multiple ways.

* Deal flow: Direct referrals from relationships.
* Pricing: Better terms from relationship credibility.
* Speed: Faster closings from relationship efficiency.
* Access: Earlier visibility into opportunities.

The components vary by relationship type and quality.

## The Calculation Framework

Relationship ROI requires systematic calculation.

+ Track investment by relationship type.
+ Track return by relationship source.
+ Calculate ROI by relationship category.
+ Optimize investment allocation.

The framework reveals which relationships generate returns.

## The Optimization Strategy

Optimization improves relationship economics.

* Invest more in high-ROI relationships.
* Reduce investment in low-ROI relationships.
* Expand relationships with potential.
* Terminate relationships without return.

The strategy maximizes return on relationship investment.
