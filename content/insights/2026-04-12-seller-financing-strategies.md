Seller financing affects deal economics, alignment, and execution probability. Buyers who understand seller financing strategies achieve better terms. This memo analyzes seller financing strategies.

## The Seller Financing Rationale

Seller financing involves seller taking debt or equity in the acquired business.

+ Reduces buyer capital requirements.
+ Creates seller alignment with buyer success.
+ Signals seller confidence in business sustainability.
+ Provides flexible terms unavailable from third parties.

Seller financing enables deals that third-party financing cannot support.

## Financing Structure Options

Seller financing takes multiple structural forms.

+ Subordinated debt: seller-lender position behind senior debt. Lower risk, lower return.
+ Convertible debt: debt that converts to equity under specified conditions. Upside participation.
+ Equity rollover: seller maintains equity stake in combined entity. Maximum alignment.
+ Earnout with seller financing: contingent payments financed by seller.

Structure selection depends on alignment goals, seller risk tolerance, and tax considerations.

## The Alignment Effect

Seller equity rollover creates maximum alignment.

+ Seller maintains economic exposure to business.
+ Seller motivated to support transition.
+ Seller retains knowledge and relationships.

Equity rollover often produces the best post-acquisition outcomes when sellers are capable and willing to remain involved.

## Risk Considerations

Seller financing creates specific risks for buyers.

+ Seller priority in liquidation: seller debt may have priority over buyer equity.
+ Seller interference: retained equity creates voice in operations.
+ Seller departure: if seller leaves, business may lose value.
+ Tax implications: seller financing has specific tax treatment.

Risk assessment should inform structure selection.

## Tax Considerations

Seller financing has significant tax implications.

+ Installment sale treatment: income recognition may be deferred.
+ Interest income: seller recognizes interest income.
+ Capital gains: seller recognizes capital gains at closing or over time.

Tax advisors should structure seller financing to optimize tax outcomes for both parties.

## Negotiating Seller Financing

Seller financing requires specific negotiation elements.

+ Interest rate: market rate or below-market for alignment.
+ Subordination:senior or junior to bank debt.
+ Security: collateral for seller debt.
+ Covenants: restrictions on business operations.

Negotiation should balance seller terms against third-party financing terms.

## When Seller Financing Works

Seller financing works best in specific situations.

+ Quality businesses with strong fundamentals.
+ Sellers willing to remain involved post-closing.
+ Valuation gaps that require creative structuring.
+ Limited third-party financing availability.

Seller financing is not appropriate for all deals.
