Escrow and holdback mechanisms protect buyers and sellers in acquisition transactions. Properly structured mechanisms facilitate deals while protecting against post-closing issues. This memo analyzes escrow and holdback strategies.

## The Escrow Rationale

Escrow mechanisms provide security for post-closing obligations.

+ Protects buyer against unknown liabilities.
+ Protects seller against false claims.
+ Facilitates deal completion when risks exist.
+ Creates mechanism for dispute resolution.

Escrow bridges gaps between buyer and seller risk tolerance.

## Escrow Types

Multiple escrow types serve different purposes.

+ General holdback: percentage of purchase price held for general representations.
+ Specific escrow: funds held for specific identified risks.
+ Working capital escrow: funds held for working capital true-up.
+ Indemnity escrow: funds held for potential indemnity claims.

Type selection depends on deal-specific risks.

## Escrow Sizing

Escrow size should reflect risk profile.

+ Percentage of purchase price: typical range 10-25%.
+ Risk-based sizing: larger escrow for higher risk deals.
+ Negotiated sizing: balance between protection and seller economics.

Sizing should match actual risk exposure.

## Escrow Periods

Escrow periods should match risk timelines.

+ Short-term escrow: 6-12 months for general representations.
+ Medium-term escrow: 12-24 months for specific risks.
+ Long-term escrow: multi-year for environmental or tax risks.

Periods should match statute of limitations for specific risks.

## Release Mechanisms

Escrow release mechanisms should be clear.

+ Time-based release: automatic release after period expiration.
+ Claim-based release: release subject to pending claims.
+ Approval-based release: release requires mutual approval.

Clear mechanisms prevent disputes.

## Working Capital Escrows

Working capital escrows address specific issues.

+ True-up mechanism: adjust for working capital at closing.
+ Holdback for adjustment: funds held until final determination.
+ Dispute resolution: mechanism for disagreement resolution.

Working capital escrows prevent post-closing disputes.

## Escrow Agent Selection

Escrow agent selection affects execution.

+ Third-party agent: bank or escrow company provides independence.
+ Attorney escrow: attorney provides escrow services.
+ wire instructions: ensure proper controls.

Selection should consider expertise and cost.

## Sammanfattning

Escrow mechanisms protect both parties in transactions. Multiple types serve different purposes. Sizing should match risk profile. Periods should match risk timelines. Release mechanisms should be clear. Escrow agent selection affects execution.
