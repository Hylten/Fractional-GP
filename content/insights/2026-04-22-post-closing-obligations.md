Post-closing obligations continue after transaction completion. Buyers who manage obligations avoid surprises. This memo analyzes post-closing obligations.

## Obligation Categories

Multiple categories require ongoing attention.

+ Indemnification: seller indemnification obligations.
+ Escrow: escrow release and claims.
+ Earnout: earnout payments.
+ Employee matters: employment obligations.
+ Tax matters: tax filing and payment.

Categories require different management.

## Indemnification Management

Indemnification requires specific management.

+ Track claims: monitor for claims.
+ Respond to claims: address claims appropriately.
+ Escrow release: manage release process.
+ Resolution: resolve disputes.

Management prevents disputes.

## Earnout Management

Earnouts require specific management.

+ Track performance: monitor metrics.
+ Communicate: maintain seller relationship.
+ Calculate amounts: determine payments.
+ Resolve disputes: address disagreements.

Management maintains relationships.

## Escrow Management

Escrow requires specific management.

+ Track periods: monitor escrow time.
+ File claims: submit claims if necessary.
+ Release funds: process releases.
+ Resolve disputes: address disagreements.

Management protects interests.

## Employee Obligations

Employee obligations continue post-closing.

+ Retention payments: make required payments.
+ Benefits: maintain benefits.
+ Employment terms: honor agreements.
+ Non-competes: enforce non-competes.

Management maintains compliance.

## Tax Obligations

Tax obligations require ongoing attention.

+ Filings: file required returns.
+ Payments: make required payments.
+ Audits: respond to audits.
+ Amendments: amend if necessary.

Management prevents penalties.

## Records Retention

Records retention supports obligations.

+ Maintain records: keep required records.
+ Organize: organize for access.
+ Retention periods: understand requirements.
+ Destruction: destroy when appropriate.

Retention supports defense.

## Sammanfattning

Post-closing obligations require ongoing management. Indemnification requires claim management. Earnout requires performance tracking. Escrow requires release management. Employee obligations require compliance. Tax obligations require filing. Records retention supports obligations.
