Single-channel deal sourcing misses opportunities that multi-channel approaches capture. The firms that orchestrate outreach across platforms access more deals at lower cost.

## The Channel Portfolio

Each channel has distinct characteristics, costs, and conversion dynamics.

> Direct mail reaches owners who check physical mail. The channel has high visibility per piece but limited scalability. Best for high-value targets.

> Email reaches owners who respond to digital outreach. The channel has scalability but lower response rates. Best for volume approaches.

> Phone calls reach owners who prefer conversation. The channel has highest conversion but highest cost. Best for high-priority targets.

> Social platforms reach owners who maintain professional presence. The channel has relationship-building potential. Best for long-cycle engagement.

The channel portfolio balances coverage, cost, and conversion.

## The Orchestration Logic

Channel orchestration requires logic for when to use each channel.

* Initial contact often uses letter or email for low-cost introduction.
* Follow-up uses different channel to create additional touchpoint.
* High-priority targets receive phone outreach for velocity.
* Long-term nurture uses social channels for relationship building.

The orchestration creates multiple opportunities for engagement. The target has multiple chances to respond. The firm has multiple ways to demonstrate value.

+ Map channels to stages in the deal flow process.
+ Optimize channel selection based on conversion data.
+ Test new channels for expansion.
+ De-prioritize channels that underperform.

## The Integration Architecture

Channel data must integrate into unified view.

* All channel interactions logged in CRM.
* Response tracking across channels.
* Attribution for conversion.
* Optimization for channel mix.

The integration enables cross-channel optimization. The firm understands which channels contribute to which outcomes.

## The Multi-Touch Reality

Most deals require multiple touches across multiple channels.

* First touch may generate awareness.
* Second touch may generate consideration.
* Third touch may generate engagement.
* Fourth touch may generate conversion.

The firms that optimize for multi-touch convert more deals. The firms that give up after single touch lose opportunities.
