LP diversification strategies are built on the assumption that private equity behaves like public markets. More managers. More strategies. More vintages. The assumption is wrong. Private equity diversification does not reduce risk in the ways that LPs expect.

## The Correlated Loss Problem

Private equity returns are highly correlated during market downturns. When public markets decline, private equity follows. The correlation is not 1:1 but it is substantially higher than most LPs assume.

During the 2008-2009 correction, private equity returns declined in lockstep with public markets. The diversification that LPs believed they had constructed evaporated. The protection that public market diversification provides was not available in private markets.

The correlation increases during stress. During normal markets, private equity returns may decouple from public returns. During crisis, the decoupling disappears. The diversification benefit exists when it is least needed and disappears when it is most needed.

## The Vintage Concentration Problem

LPs invest across vintages to reduce concentration risk. This strategy is based on a flawed understanding of vintage-year risk.

Vintages are not independent. A 2008 vintage and a 2009 vintage are not uncorrelated. They share market conditions at the time of investment. The 2008 vintage invested during peak pricing. The 2009 vintage invested during distressed conditions. The outcomes were very different.

The relevant risk is not vintage concentration. It is exposure to specific market conditions. LPs who diversify across vintages may still be concentrated in conditions that produce poor returns. The diversification does not address the fundamental risk.

## The Manager Concentration Problem

LPs diversify across managers to reduce manager-specific risk. This strategy fails because manager performance is persistent.

Top-quartile managers tend to stay top-quartile. Bottom-quartile managers tend to stay bottom-quartile. The correlation between past and future performance is high. Diversification across managers mixes top and bottom performers.

The LP ends up with average performance. The average of top and bottom quartile returns is middle-quartile return. Diversification converges toward the mean. The LP would be better served by identifying top managers and concentrating capital with them.

## The Strategy Concentration Problem

LPs diversify across strategies to reduce strategy risk. Buyout, growth, credit, real assets. The strategies are not uncorrelated. They share common dependence on macroeconomic conditions.

When interest rates rise, all strategies are affected. When credit conditions tighten, all strategies are affected. When recession risk increases, all strategies are affected. The diversification across strategies does not provide the protection that LP models assume.

The strategies that underperform during specific conditions may outperform during others. But the LP cannot predict which strategy will outperform in which environment. The diversification becomes a bet on conditions that cannot be forecast.

## The Recommendation

Diversification in private equity is an illusion. The correlations are higher than models assume. The persistence of manager performance defeats diversification across managers. The macro dependencies defeat diversification across strategies.

The alternative is to concentrate with top managers. Identify the GPs who generate top-quartile returns. Allocate capital to those GPs in significant size. Accept that concentration creates tracking error against benchmarks. The tracking error is the price of outperformance.

The LP who diversifies will get benchmark returns. The LP who concentrates with top managers will get outperformance. The choice is between getting average returns or getting premium returns. The diversification narrative serves average returns.

Build relationships with top managers. Maintain conviction through market cycles. Accept concentration as the cost of outperformance. The portfolio construction that achieves superior returns is concentrated, not diversified.
