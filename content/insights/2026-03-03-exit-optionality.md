Exit optionality determines returns. Deal flow quality affects exit options. The firms that consider exits during acquisition generate better outcomes.

## The Exit Options

Multiple exit paths exist for private acquisitions.

> Trade sale to strategic buyer.
> Secondary sale to financial buyer.
> IPO for appropriate businesses.
> Recapitalization for continued ownership.

The exit options vary by business characteristics.

## The Acquisition Influence

Acquisition characteristics affect exit options.

* Sector focus affects strategic buyer interest.
* Business model affects financial buyer interest.
* Growth profile affects IPO viability.
* Management depth affects recapitalization potential.

The acquisition decision is also an exit decision.

## The Optionality Building

Optionality should be built into acquisitions.

+ Acquire businesses with multiple exit paths.
+ Develop relationships with potential buyers.
* Maintain strategic optionality during hold.
* Build value that attracts buyers.

The optionality increases with preparation.

## The Timing Coordination

Exit timing coordinates with market timing.

> Sell into strong markets.
> Hold through weak markets.
> Prepare exit processes in advance.
* Time exit for maximum value.

The timing requires discipline and preparation.
