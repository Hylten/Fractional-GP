Acquired businesses become deal flow engines. The platform creates touchpoints, relationships, and intelligence that generate future deals.

## The Platform Multiplier

Every acquired business creates multiple deal flow channels.

* Supplier relationships provide market intelligence.
* Customer relationships reveal consolidation opportunities.
* Employee networks spread reputation.
* Industry presence generates inbound inquiries.

The platform multiplier generates deal flow that pure sourcing efforts cannot match.

## The Acquisition Advantage

Acquisitions provide competitive advantages that sourcing cannot replicate.

> Acquired businesses have existing relationships with owners considering exit.
> Acquired businesses have industry credibility that accelerates new deals.
> Acquired businesses have operational expertise that improves execution.
> Acquired businesses have infrastructure that reduces cost of new deals.

The acquisition advantage compounds. Each deal improves capability for the next deal.

## The Roll-Up Logic

Roll-up strategies systematically acquire platform businesses.

* Identify fragmented sectors.
* Acquire platform businesses in each geography.
* Use platform relationships to source additional acquisitions.
* Build dominant market position.

The roll-up logic converts deal flow into market power. The power generates additional deal flow.

+ The platform creates value.
+ The value attracts capital.
+ The capital enables more platforms.
+ More platforms generate more deal flow.

## The Long-Term View

Platform strategies require long-term investment.

* Platforms take years to build relationships.
* Returns compound over extended periods.
* Short-term thinking undermines platform value.
* Long-term commitment enables transformation.

The firms that think in platform terms build durable advantages. The firms that think transactionally miss the compounding benefit.
