Deal flow intelligence flows through advisor networks before it flows through brokers or listings. Buyers who build strategic advisor relationships access deals earlier, with better information, and at lower cost than those who rely on public channels. This memo outlines the advisor triangulation strategy.

## The Advisor Information Economy

Business advisors operate at the intersection of seller motivation and transaction timing. Accountants learn about succession planning when preparing financial statements. Attorneys hear about partnership disputes during entity structuring. Insurance brokers discover coverage gaps that signal operational stress. Bankers observe cash flow patterns that indicate growth challenges or capital needs.

This information flow gives advisors advance warning of transaction intent that sellers do not share with brokers or financial buyers. The warning window typically ranges from 6-18 months before a formal sale process begins. Buyers who access this information through advisor relationships can engage sellers during the optimal early phase of the selling cycle.

The economic structure of advisory relationships creates natural incentives for information sharing. Advisors earn fees from transaction support, but they also maintain long-term client relationships that generate recurring revenue. Recommending a credible buyer who closes cleanly protects the advisor's client relationship while generating referral fees. The alignment of interests between advisor, seller, and buyer creates sustainable information channels.

## The CPA Strategy

Certified public accountants represent the highest-value advisor relationship for deal sourcing. CPA firms serve as the financial backbone for private companies, maintaining intimate knowledge of business performance, owner situations, and succession timelines.

The relationship building approach differs from traditional business development. CPAs receive constant inbound requests from buyers seeking referrals. Differentiation requires demonstrating credibility and alignment with client interests. The most effective approach involves providing value to CPAs without immediate expectation of return.

+ Share industry insights and market intelligence that CPAs can use to serve their clients better.
* Offer to speak at CPA firm events on topics relevant to their business owner clients.
* Provide educational content about transaction structuring, tax optimization, and exit planning.
- Create referral arrangements that benefit both the CPA firm and individual practitioners.

The CPA relationship requires patience and consistency. Transaction opportunities may take 12-24 months to materialize from initial relationship building. However, the quality of CPA referrals typically exceeds broker-sourced deals because CPAs understand their clients' motivations and can match with appropriate buyers.

## Attorney Network Development

Business attorneys, particularly those specializing in transactions, employment, or intellectual property, maintain visibility into company events that precede sales. Partnership dissolutions, key employee departures, or IP disputes often precede strategic transitions that lead to sales.

Building attorney relationships requires understanding the referral economics of law firms. Partners make referral decisions based on client service quality and potential for future work. Buyers must demonstrate capability to close transactions smoothly, protecting the attorney's client relationship and positioning for future referrals.

The attorney channel complements the CPA channel. Attorneys typically become involved later in the selling process than accountants, meaning attorney relationships provide mid-cycle intelligence while CPA relationships provide early signals. triangulation between both channels creates comprehensive coverage.

## Banker and Lender Relationships

Commercial bankers and alternative lenders observe financial stress or opportunity through loan monitoring and credit relationships. Businesses approaching covenant issues or seeking growth capital often discuss alternatives with their lenders before engaging brokers or buyers.

Lender relationships require demonstrating financing capability and transaction credibility. Banks prefer to maintain lending relationships through ownership transitions rather than writing off troubled loans. A buyer who can assume existing financing or provide clean take-out financing becomes a preferred solution for lenders managing troubled relationships.

The lender channel provides access to businesses in financial distress or transition. These opportunities often carry higher risk but also higher return potential. Buyers with operational improvement capability can create significant value from lender-instigated transactions.

## Building the Network Structure

Effective advisor networks require systematic cultivation rather than ad hoc relationship management.

* Create a target advisor list for each sector and geography of interest.
+ Prioritize advisors based on client base quality and referral history.
- Schedule regular touchpoints with key advisors, even without immediate opportunities.
* Develop referral protocols that make it easy for advisors to identify and communicate potential opportunities.
- Measure advisor channel performance and concentrate relationships on highest-producing advisors.

The network structure should include redundancy. Relying on a single CPA or attorney creates vulnerability to relationship disruption. Building 3-5 relationships in each advisor category provides coverage and competitive dynamics.

## Channel Economics

Advisor-sourced deals typically exhibit superior economics compared to broker-sourced or listed deals. The advantages manifest across multiple dimensions.

+ Earlier seller engagement produces higher motivation and better pricing.
* Less competition reduces auction dynamics and their price-increasing effects.
- More complete information from advisor context improves due diligence efficiency.
- Advisor credibility transfer improves seller trust and negotiation dynamics.

The economics justify investment in relationship building. The time value of advisor network development typically produces 200-400 basisives improvement in entry multiples compared to broker-sourced deals. The improvement compounds across portfolio returns.

## Operational Integration

Advisor channel development should integrate with overall sourcing strategy.

+ Track advisor relationships in CRM with activity history and referral potential.
* Create sector-specific advisor networks aligned with acquisition focus.
- Develop materials that communicate acquisition criteria and process to advisors.
* Establish clear protocols for responding to advisor referrals with speed and professionalism.

Speed matters in advisor relationships. When an advisor refers a motivated seller, the buyer who responds first and professionally captures the opportunity. Delays signal lack of seriousness and damage future referral potential.

## Sammanfattning

Advisor networks provide deal flow advantages that public channels cannot replicate. CPAs, attorneys, lenders, and other advisors maintain visibility into seller situations before formal marketing begins. Building strategic relationships with these advisors requires patience, credibility, and systematic cultivation. The returns from advisor-sourced deals justify the relationship investment through earlier engagement, better information, and reduced competition.
