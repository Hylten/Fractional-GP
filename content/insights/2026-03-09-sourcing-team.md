Sourcing capability lives in people. Building the team builds the capability. The team that compounds knowledge creates advantage that cannot be easily replicated.

## The Capability Components

Sourcing capability requires multiple skill sets.

> Relationship skills: Building rapport with owners and advisors.
> Analytical skills: Evaluating opportunities quickly.
> Process skills: Managing pipeline and follow-up.
> Domain expertise: Understanding sector dynamics.

The capability components must be present in the team. Weakness in any component limits overall performance.

## The Specialization Benefits

Specialization improves performance.

* Dedicated sourcers focus on outreach.
* Dedicated analysts focus on evaluation.
* Dedicated relationship managers focus on advisor networks.
* Dedicated sector experts focus on domain knowledge.

Specialization develops depth. Depth creates expertise. Expertise generates advantage.

## The Knowledge Capture

Team knowledge must be captured and preserved.

* Pipeline data provides historical patterns.
* Process documentation enables replication.
* Outcome tracking enables optimization.
* Institutional memory prevents knowledge loss.

The capture system ensures continuity. Personnel changes do not destroy capability. The firm retains knowledge.

## The Compounding Effect

Effective teams compound knowledge over time.

+ Each deal teaches something.
+ Each interaction reveals patterns.
+ Each success creates relationships.
+ Each failure provides insight.

The compounding effect makes teams more valuable over time. The firm that invests in team development captures the compounding benefit.
