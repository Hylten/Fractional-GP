Customer concentration significantly affects business value and risk. Buyers who properly assess concentration risk avoid value destruction. This memo analyzes customer concentration analysis.

## The Concentration Risk Framework

Customer concentration measures revenue dependency on specific customers.

+ Single customer concentration: revenue from single customer.
+ Top customer concentration: revenue from largest customers.
+ Industry concentration: revenue from specific industries.
+ Geographic concentration: revenue from specific regions.

Concentration creates asymmetric risk: losing a major customer has larger impact than gaining a similar customer.

## Valuation Impact

Customer concentration directly affects business valuation.

+ High concentration reduces valuation multiples.
+ Customer concentration risk is difficult to insure.
+ Concentration affects financing terms.
+ Concentration affects strategic buyer interest.

Valuation should reflect concentration risk appropriately.

## Due Diligence Requirements

Customer concentration due diligence requires specific analysis.

+ Customer contracts: terms, duration, renewal rates.
+ Customer relationships: depth, history, satisfaction.
+ Customer financials: customer financial health.
+ Customer strategy: customer procurement direction.
+ Competitive positioning: relationship sustainability.

Due diligence should assess both current concentration and trajectory.

## Concentration Risk Categories

Concentration risk varies by customer type.

+ Government contracts: valuable but risky due to procurement changes.
+ Large corporate customers: powerful but may have relationship longevity.
+ Franchise relationships: structured but often have concentration protections.
+ Key account relationships: valuable but often informal.

Understanding customer type enables appropriate risk assessment.

## Risk Mitigation Strategies

Customer concentration risk can be mitigated through multiple strategies.

+ Diversification programs: systematically reduce concentration.
+ Contract enhancements: longer terms, renewal provisions.
+ Relationship investment: deepen relationships beyond transactional.
+ Customer acquisition: expand customer base.

Mitigation programs create value and reduce risk.

## Post-Acquisition Planning

Post-acquisition customer retention requires specific planning.

+ Key contact identification and relationship building.
+ Communication planning to ensure smooth transition.
+ Service level maintenance during transition.
+ Quick wins to demonstrate value.

Post-acquisition customer loss destroys value quickly.

## The Growth Paradox

Growth can increase concentration risk paradoxically.

+ Rapid growth with concentrated customers increases exposure.
+ Success with major customers may reduce diversification incentive.
+ Industry dynamics may increase concentration over time.

Growth planning should consider concentration trajectory.

## Sammanfattning

Customer concentration significantly affects business value and risk. Valuation should reflect concentration appropriately. Due diligence should assess relationship depth and sustainability. Risk varies by customer type. Mitigation strategies can reduce concentration over time. Post-acquisition retention planning is critical. Growth can paradoxically increase concentration risk.
