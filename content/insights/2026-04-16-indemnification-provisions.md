Indemnification provisions allocate post-closing risk between buyer and seller. Properly structured indemnification protects both parties. This memo analyzes indemnification provisions.

## The Indemnification Framework

Indemnification provisions create obligations for post-closing liabilities.

+ Seller indemnifies buyer for specified liabilities.
+ Buyer indemnifies seller for post-closing obligations.
+ Survival periods define how long indemnification survives.
+ Caps and baskets limit exposure.

Indemnification allocates risk that due diligence cannot fully identify.

## Common Indemnification Categories

Specific indemnification categories appear in most transactions.

+ Representations and warranties: accuracy of seller representations.
+ Pre-closing liabilities: undisclosed or unknown liabilities.
+ Tax liabilities: pre-closing tax obligations.
+ Environmental liabilities: environmental issues discovered post-closing.
+ Key employee matters: departure or dispute.

Categories should reflect transaction-specific risks.

## Survival Periods

Different representations have different survival periods.

+ Fundamental representations: often survive indefinitely or longest period.
+ General representations: typically survive 12-24 months.
+ Tax representations: often survive for statute of limitations.
+ Environmental representations: often survive 3-5 years or longer.

Survival periods should match risk profile.

## Caps and Baskets

Indemnification typically includes caps and baskets.

+ Caps: maximum indemnification amount, often percentage of purchase price.
+ Baskets: minimum amount before indemnification triggers, often percentage of price.
+ Deductibles: buyer bears losses up to basket amount.

Caps and baskets balance risk allocation with seller retention.

## Carve-Outs

Specific items typically carve out from limitations.

+ Fraud: intentional misrepresentation.
+ Key representations: fundamental representations.
+ Known issues: specifically identified liabilities.

Carve-outs ensure protection for material issues.

## Escrow Mechanisms

Escrow provides security for indemnification claims.

+ Holdback escrow: portion of purchase price held post-closing.
+ Specific escrow: escrow for specific known risks.
+ Insurance-backed escrow: escrow backed by representations insurance.

Escrow mechanism selection depends on deal structure.

## Representations Insurance

Representations insurance transfers risk to insurers.

+ Covers breaches of representations and warranties.
+ Provides coverage beyond seller indemnification.
+ Requires clean representations and due diligence.
+ Premium cost affects deal economics.

Representations insurance may replace seller indemnification for mature deals.

## Negotiation Dynamics

Indemnification negotiation reflects risk allocation.

+ Buyer seeks broad, long survival indemnification.
+ Seller seeks limited, short survival indemnification.
+ Market practice establishes baseline expectations.
+ Deal-specific risks affect customization.

Negotiation should reflect actual risk profile.

## Sammanfattning

Indemnification provisions allocate post-closing risk. Survival periods vary by representation type. Caps and baskets limit exposure. Carve-outs protect material issues. Escrow provides security. Representations insurance provides transfer. Negotiation reflects risk allocation.
