Sourcing efficiency determines acquisition program success. Buyers who apply systematic data approaches to deal identification outperform those who rely on intuition and relationships. This memo analyzes data-driven sourcing methodologies.

## The Sourcing Efficiency Imperative

Acquisition programs succeed or fail based on sourcing efficiency. The ratio of deals sourced to deals completed determines capital deployment velocity. Capital deployment velocity affects returns through the compounding effect of deployed capital versus idle capital.

Traditional sourcing relies on relationships, broker networks, and intuition. These approaches work but have limitations. Relationship-based sourcing is limited by network size. Broker-based sourcing introduces middlemen who represent seller interests. Intuition-based sourcing is not scalable or systematic.

Data-driven sourcing extends reach, systematizes identification, and enables scaling. The buyer who builds data capabilities achieves superior sourcing efficiency.

## Data Source Categories

Multiple data sources enable systematic deal identification.

+ Public databases: SEC filings, business registrations, professional licenses. Identify businesses by sector, size, location.
- Financial data providers: credit data, financial statement databases. Identify businesses by financial performance.
- Contact databases: professional contact information, organizational data. Enable direct outreach.
- News and media: monitoring for events that signal potential transitions.
- Social media: LinkedIn tracking for career changes, company announcements.

Data source diversity enables comprehensive coverage. Single sources provide partial coverage; multiple sources enable triangulation.

## Screening Criteria Development

Systematic screening requires clear criteria that define target characteristics.

+ Sector criteria: specific industries or sub-industries. Focus enables expertise development.
- Size criteria: revenue or EBITDA ranges. Size alignment enables appropriate financing.
- Geography criteria: specific markets or regions. Geographic focus enables relationship building.
- Performance criteria: profitability thresholds, growth rates. Performance criteria filter for quality.

Criteria should be specific enough to focus effort but broad enough to maintain deal flow volume.

## The Scoring Framework

Deal screening should produce scores that prioritize effort.

+ Motivation scoring: indicators of seller willingness to engage. LinkedIn posts, advisor relationships, timing signals.
- Fit scoring: alignment with acquisition criteria. Sector, size, geography, performance match.
- Access scoring: ease of reaching decision-makers. Contact availability, relationship proximity.
- Competitive scoring: likelihood of competition. Strategic buyer interest, market activity.

Scoring enables efficient resource allocation. High-scoring opportunities receive priority attention.

## Outreach Optimization

Data enables outreach optimization beyond initial identification.

+ Contact accuracy: verify contact information before outreach. Accurate data improves response rates.
- Personalization signals: identify personalization opportunities from data. Common connections, shared characteristics.
- Timing optimization: identify optimal outreach timing. Event-based triggers, seasonal patterns.
- Channel selection: select appropriate outreach channel. Email, LinkedIn, direct mail based on contact data.

Data-driven outreach outperforms generic outreach. The efficiency differential compounds across large outreach volumes.

## Continuous Improvement

Data systems should enable continuous improvement in sourcing efficiency.

+ Tracking: capture outreach response and conversion data. Understand what works.
+ Analysis: identify patterns in successful versus unsuccessful sourcing. Data reveals insights.
+ Optimization: adjust criteria, scoring, and outreach based on analysis. Close the learning loop.

Continuous improvement turns sourcing from art to science over time. The compounding learning effect creates sustainable advantage.

## Technology Infrastructure

Data-driven sourcing requires appropriate technology infrastructure.

+ CRM systems: manage relationships, track interactions, capture data.
+ Outreach tools: automate outreach sequences, track responses, manage follow-ups.
+ Data platforms: aggregate data from multiple sources, enable analysis.
+ Enrichment services: enhance data with additional attributes, contact information.

Technology investment pays returns through sourcing efficiency improvement. The appropriate scale of investment depends on acquisition program size.

## Sammanfattning

Data-driven sourcing enables systematic deal identification that outperforms intuition-based approaches. Multiple data source categories enable comprehensive coverage. Screening criteria development focuses effort on highest-probability targets. Scoring frameworks prioritize resource allocation. Outreach optimization improves conversion rates. Continuous improvement creates compounding learning effects. Technology infrastructure enables data-driven approaches at scale.
