AI Deal Origination: The Machine Learning Systems Transforming Acquisitions

December 11, 2025 8 min read • Jonas Hyltén

The Rules-Based Limitation

Traditional deal origination relies on rules-based filtering. Deal teams define criteria. Systems apply criteria. Targets meeting criteria advance. Targets failing criteria drop. The rules produce consistent but limited results.

The limitation intensifies as markets evolve. Rules that worked historically may not predict future success. Static criteria cannot adapt to changing conditions. The inflexibility creates blind spots that AI overcomes.

AI deal origination transforms filtering to prediction. The systems learn from outcomes, adapt to market changes, and identify patterns rules cannot detect. The intelligence generates advantages that rules-based approaches cannot achieve.

A Swedish industrial acquirer demonstrates the limitation impact. Rules-based filtering produced 200 targets monthly. Analyst review time consumed 80 hours monthly. Conversion rate sat at 4 percent. AI implementation produced 350 targets monthly. Review time reduced to 30 hours monthly. Conversion improved to 11 percent. The AI investment generated measurable returns within six months.
