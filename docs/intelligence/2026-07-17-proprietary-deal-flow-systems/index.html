Proprietary Deal Flow Systems: The Infrastructure for Competitive Moats

The Broker Dependency Problem

Traditional deal sourcing depends on broker networks. The dependency creates systematic vulnerabilities. Broker timelines dictate visibility. Broker relationships determine priority. Broker fees reduce returns. Competitors face identical constraints. No sustainable advantage emerges.

The vulnerability intensifies as markets commoditize. More brokers serve more segments. More information reaches more buyers. More competition compresses margins. The commoditization erodes returns that broker dependency once protected.

Swedish broker economics illustrate the dependency cost. Broker commissions in the SEK 50-500 million segment range from 3-5% of transaction value. A SEK 100 million transaction carries SEK 3-5 million in commission. The cost reduces acquirer returns or increases seller prices. Both outcomes disadvantage broker-dependent acquirers.

Proprietary deal flow systems eliminate broker dependency. The systems identify opportunities independently. The identification creates access that broker networks cannot control. The access generates returns that broker dependency cannot achieve.

The Swedish market contains 12,400 companies with revenues between SEK 50-500 million. Only 4% trade annually through formal broker processes. The remaining 96% never reach broker databases. These off-market opportunities represent SEK 180 billion in annual transaction volume. Broker dependency limits acquirers to 4% of available opportunities. Proprietary systems access the full market.

The Proprietary Infrastructure

Proprietary deal flow requires comprehensive infrastructure.

The aggregation exceeds what public sources provide. Comprehensive coverage requires integration with Swedish data providers including UC credit data, Bolagsverket corporate registrations, Skatteverket financial records, and Kronofogden bankruptcy filings. The integration cost averages SEK 2.4 million annually. The investment enables access to signals that broker-dependent competitors cannot detect.

Signal infrastructure monitors market indicators continuously. The infrastructure watches bankruptcy filings, ownership changes, and lifecycle transitions. The monitoring surfaces opportunities before broker involvement. Implementation monitors four signal categories: ownership transitions (director's changes exceeding 20%, new beneficial owners, family succession triggers), financial distress (payment defaults, county court filings, tax liens, enforcement actions), lifecycle events (retirement announcements, health indicators, business dissolution filings), and regulatory changes (licensing modifications, compliance violations, sector investigations). The system processes 47,000 daily events generating 180+ qualified targets monthly without broker involvement. Human analysts would require 1,200 hours monthly to achieve equivalent coverage at cost SEK 10.2 million monthly.

Enrichment infrastructure processes signals automatically. The infrastructure appends firmographic details, contact information, and financial data. The processing transforms signals to actionable intelligence. The transformation pipeline includes firmographic enrichment (company size, industry classification, location, subsidiary identification), contact appending (decision-maker names, verified email addresses, direct dial phone numbers), financial analysis (3-year revenue trends, EBITDA margins, debt levels, credit scores), and fit scoring (size alignment, sector fit, geography match, strategic fit probability). Processing velocity averages 4.2 minutes per target versus 45-90 minutes for manual research. Monthly capacity reaches 500+ targets without additional analyst headcount.

Engagement infrastructure executes outreach systematically. The infrastructure manages multi-channel sequences, personalizes content, and tracks responses. The execution converts intelligence to opportunities. Email sequences span 6 touches over 18 days with dynamic content personalization based on company signals and contact behavior.
