Off-market transactions offer superior economics compared to competitive bid processes. Information Asymmetry benefits sellers preferring confidential transitions. Buyers access opportunities unavailable through broker channels. Principal investors prioritize off-market sourcing for strategic advantage. The economics of off-market deals justify systematic investment.

Competitive processes inflate acquisition costs. Multiple bidders drive prices toward fair market value. Off-market negotiations enable value capture through asymmetric information. Deal flow dominance requires systematic off-market identification capability. Access creates advantage.
