Sourcing Excellence: The Art of Deal Identification

March 22, 2026 8 min read • Jonas Hyltén

The Sourcing Imperative

Sourcing determines acquisition program potential that every other capability builds upon. No program succeeds without deal flow to convert into transactions. The sourcing function establishes the foundation upon which all acquisition value creation rests. Programs that invest in sourcing excellence outperform those that neglect this foundational capability. Sourcing determines ceiling. A Swedish industrial platform invested 85 MSEK in sourcing capability over 6 years. Deal flow increased 280%. Conversion improved 34%. Value created exceeded 320 MSEK annually. Sourcing generates returns.

Sourcing quality affects every subsequent process stage dramatically. High-quality targets convert efficiently through evaluation without consuming excessive resources. Low-quality targets consume resources without generating returns that justify the investment. The sourcing discipline filters poor candidates early before significant resources commit. Quality determines efficiency. Quality determines economics.

Competitive dynamics intensify sourcing requirements as markets become more sophisticated. Multiple acquirers pursue similar targets with increasing sophistication. Superior sourcing enables access that competitors forfeit through inadequate approaches. The sourcing advantage translates directly to acquisition economics through better pricing and terms. Advantage creates value. Access enables economics.

Systematic approaches outperform opportunistic ones consistently over time. Programs with defined processes generate reliable deal flow regardless of market conditions. Programs relying on chance generate unpredictable results that planning could prevent. The systematic discipline separates successful programs from unsuccessful ones. Systematic enables reliability. Reliability enables planning.

Target Universe Definition

Target universe definition establishes the population from which deals emerge. The definition must balance specificity against volume sufficiency. Overly narrow definitions produce insufficient pipeline that cannot support portfolio building. Overly broad definitions disperses resources across too many poor candidates. Balance determines efficiency. Definition determines effectiveness.

Strategic criteria define ideal target characteristics that align with portfolio objectives. Sector focus concentrates effort where organizational expertise exists. Size range ensures targets match capital availability and operational capacity. Geographic scope aligns with organizational presence and knowledge. The criteria must reflect portfolio strategy and organizational capabilities. Criteria enable targeting. Targeting enables efficiency.

Market mapping reveals the complete opportunity landscape systematically. Competitor analysis identifies adjacent players that extend market reach. Industry structure analysis reveals consolidation opportunities in fragmented markets. Technology assessment identifies capability gaps that acquisitions could fill. The mapping approach ensures comprehensive coverage that opportunistic identification cannot match. Mapping enables coverage. Coverage enables access.

Prioritization ranks the mapped universe by attractiveness and accessibility. Highest-priority targets receive immediate attention and resources. Lower-priority targets queue for future engagement as capacity allows. The prioritization discipline focuses resources on highest-value opportunities that justify intensive investment. Prioritization enables focus. Focus enables efficiency.

Content updates expose strategic pivots that owners have not announced. The website activity provides real-time intelligence on target developments. Monitoring enables early detection. Detection enables access.

Leadership change tracking monitors management transitions that often precede strategic changes. CEO departures signal strategic uncertainty that might create sale opportunity. New hires indicate capability priorities that reveal strategic direction. The leadership activity reveals organizational dynamics that affect transaction readiness. Leadership changes precede transactions. Changes create opportunity.

Funding and investment tracking monitors capital activity that affects transaction timing. Follow-on funding pressures indicate growth challenges that might create sale motivation. Pressure creates access.

Network development requires sustained investment that short-term focus cannot justify. Network enables access. Investment enables advantage.

Attorneys advise founders on succession planning that often leads to transactions. Accountants observe owner exhaustion that precedes sale decisions. The advisor relationships provide early warning that competitors lack. Advisors provide early access. Access enables economics.

The service provider networks provide visibility that direct relationships cannot match. Service providers observe dynamics. Dynamics reveal opportunity.

Context enables positioning.

Consistent deal execution generates referrals that expand sourcing reach. The referral network compounds over successful transactions as positive experiences spread. Reputation generates referrals. Referrals provide quality access.

Engagement Strategy Design

Engagement strategy guides how targets convert to prospects that eventually become deals. The strategy must balance relationship development pace against resource availability. Rushed engagement damages relationships that take time to build properly. Delayed engagement misses windows that competitors capture. Balance determines success. Timing determines conversion.

Approach sequencing defines engagement progression that builds relationship over time. Initial contact establishes credibility without demanding commitment. Follow-up engagement provides value through market intelligence and strategic insight. Advanced engagement explores possibilities without specific transaction pressure. The sequence must progress naturally without forcing premature transaction discussion. Sequence builds trust. Trust enables access.

Message personalization demonstrates genuine interest that generic messages cannot achieve. Generic messages generate minimal response from recipients who receive many similar communications. Personalized messages demonstrate research and relevance that warrant attention. The personalization depth must balance customization against scale constraints that limit individualization. Personalization improves response. Response enables conversion.

Channel selection matches engagement to target preferences that vary across populations. Email sequences provide primary outreach that most professionals check regularly. LinkedIn engagement extends reach that email alone cannot achieve. Phone calls accelerate response for urgent situations. The channel mix must match what works for specific populations and industries. Channel affects response. Response determines conversion.

Follow-up discipline maintains engagement momentum that inconsistent attention loses. Inconsistent follow-up loses momentum that competitors capture while attention lapses. The follow-up system ensures continuous relationship advancement toward transaction readiness. Discipline maintains momentum. Momentum enables conversion.
