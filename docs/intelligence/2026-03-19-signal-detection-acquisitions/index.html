Signal Detection for Acquisitions: The Intelligence Systems Finding Deal Opportunities

March 19, 2026 9 min read • Jonas Hyltén

The Reactive Pursuit Problem

Traditional acquisition pursues opportunities that surface. Broker listings announce targets. Industry contacts share rumors. Market events generate announcements. The approach waits for opportunities to appear. The waiting creates competition that inflates prices.

The problem intensifies as markets mature. More buyers compete for fewer opportunities. More announcements draw more competitors. More competition compresses returns. The intensity erodes returns that reactive pursuit once achieved.

Signal detection systems transform pursuit from reactive to proactive. The systems identify opportunities before announcements. The identification creates access that reactive approaches cannot match. The access improves returns that competition eliminates.

A Swedish industrial acquirer demonstrates reactive pursuit cost. Broker-sourced deals averaged 8 percent premium to intrinsic value. Time from announcement to bid averaged 14 days. Competitive processes involved 4.5 bidders average. Signal detection implementation reduced acquisition premium to 3 percent. Detection-to-bid time reduced to 3 days. Average competitive processes reduced to 2.1 bidders.
