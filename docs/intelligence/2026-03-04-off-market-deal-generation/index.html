Multiple buyers compete for broker-represented opportunities, driving up prices through competitive bidding. Price competition inflates acquisition costs beyond fundamental values. Broker relationships create information asymmetries that sophisticated sellers exploit. Brokers advise sellers on valuation and negotiation strategy. Buyers lack equivalent intelligence about seller priorities and constraints. The information advantage enables sellers to extract better terms from less-informed buyers. The asymmetry persists throughout transaction negotiations.

Off-market deal generation eliminates broker dependency entirely through direct seller relationships. The approach identifies sellers before broker involvement creates competitive pressure.
