Working Capital Normalization: Avoiding Post-Closing Surprises

April 15, 2026 • Jonas Hyltén

Working capital adjustments frequently create post-closing disputes. Buyers who understand working capital dynamics avoid surprises. This memo analyzes working capital normalization.

The Working Capital Issue

Working capital represents operating capital tied up in business operations.

+ Current assets: cash, accounts receivable, inventory.

+ Current liabilities: accounts payable, accrued expenses.

+ Working capital: current assets minus current liabilities.

Working capital normalization adjusts purchase price based on working capital levels.

Normalization Rationale

Working capital normalization serves specific purposes.

+ Ensure seller does not extract working capital pre-closing.

+ Ensure buyer receives operating working capital.

+ Align incentives between buyer and seller.

+ Provide mechanism for working capital true-up.

Normalization prevents both underpayment and overpayment for working capital.

Normalization Methodology

Working capital normalization uses specific methodology.

+ Determine normalized working capital level: trailing average, benchmark, or formula.

+ Measure working capital at closing.

+ Calculate adjustment: actual minus normalized times adjustment factor.

+ Apply adjustment to purchase price.

Methodology should be clear, objective, and fair.

Common Disputes

Working capital disputes commonly arise from specific situations.

+ Definitions: what counts as working capital.

+ Timing: when is working capital measured.

+ Adjustments: unusual items that distort measurement.

+ Benchmark selection: what represents normalized level.

Clear definitions prevent disputes.

Due Diligence Requirements

Working capital due diligence requires specific analysis.

+ Historical working capital: analyze trends and patterns.

+ Working capital drivers: understand what drives working capital.

+ Owner manipulation: assess whether working capital is optimized.

+ Benchmark comparison: compare to industry norms.

Due diligence should establish expectations for closing.

The Holdback Structure

Working capital adjustments often use holdback structures.

+ Escrow: funds held in escrow until final working capital determined.

+ Earnout adjustment: working capital affects earnout calculations.

+ Purchase price adjustment: direct adjustment to purchase price.

Structure selection depends on deal size and parties' preferences.

Post-Closing Management

Working capital management post-closing affects performance.

+ Operating working capital requirements: what level is needed for operations.

+ Working capital efficiency: optimize working capital utilization.

+ Growth working capital: capital needed for growth.

Post-closing working capital affects cash flow and value.

Sammanfattning

Working capital normalization prevents post-closing disputes. Methodology should be clear and objective. Common disputes arise from definitions, timing, and adjustments. Due diligence establishes expectations. Holdback structures provide protection. Post-closing management affects performance.
