Litigation Risk Assessment in Due Diligence

April 23, 2026 • Jonas Hyltén

Litigation risk significantly affects acquisition value. Buyers who assess litigation risk avoid value destruction. This memo analyzes litigation risk assessment.

Risk Categories

Litigation risk falls into specific categories.

+ Pending litigation: lawsuits currently filed.

+ Potential litigation: claims not yet filed.

+ Regulatory: regulatory enforcement actions.

+ Employment: employment-related claims.

+ Product liability: product-related claims.

Categories require different assessment.

Due Diligence Approach

Litigation due diligence requires specific approach.

+ Litigation search: search for pending claims.

+ Counsel review: review with legal counsel.

+ Document review: review litigation documents.

+ Assessment: assess exposure and risk.

Approach should be comprehensive.

Exposure Assessment

Exposure assessment requires specific analysis.

+ Claim assessment: evaluate claim validity.

+ Damages analysis: estimate potential damages.

+ Defense costs: estimate defense expenses.

+ Settlement probability: assess settlement likelihood.

Assessment informs valuation.

Insurance Analysis

Insurance provides risk transfer.

+ Coverage review: review existing coverage.

+ Claim history: analyze claims experience.

+ Coverage gaps: identify coverage gaps.

+ Implications: assess implications.

Insurance affects risk assessment.

Regulatory Risk

Regulatory risk requires specific assessment.

+ Regulatory status: assess regulatory standing.

+ Compliance history: review compliance history.

+ Potential actions: assess potential enforcement.

+ Exposure: estimate regulatory exposure.

Regulatory risk can be significant.

Employment Risk

Employment risk requires specific assessment.

+ Workforce issues: assess workforce issues.

+ Compliance: assess compliance status.

+ Potential claims: identify potential claims.

+ Exposure: estimate exposure.

Employment risk is often underestimated.

Risk Mitigation

Risk mitigation addresses identified issues.

+ Escrow: holdback for claims.

+ Indemnification: seller indemnification.

+ Insurance: representations insurance.

+ Adjustments: purchase price adjustments.

Mitigation transfers or reduces risk.
