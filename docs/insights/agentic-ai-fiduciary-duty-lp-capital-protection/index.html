III. Drift: The Silent Destroyer of Private Equity

Drift kills deals.

Slowly.

Quietly.

Predictably.

Drift is the deviation between expected operational behavior and actual observed performance. It is the earliest indicator of risk—and the most under-monitored domain in private equity.

I’ve observed drift destroy valuations even when headline KPIs looked “fine.”

Drift always shows up first.

Always.

Types of Drift Agentic AI Detects Instantly

Roials Alpha identifies multiple drift vectors:

- Operational drift: fulfillment delays, inventory imbalances, throughput inefficiencies

- Financial drift: cash cycle elongation, silent margin erosion

- Customer drift: rising churn risk masked by delayed reporting

- Market drift: demand seasonality shifts, competitive encroachment

- Team drift: management bandwidth decay, role dilution, underperformance

Each of these creates early warning signals that humans either miss or rationalize away.

Drift whispers.

Agents listen.

GPs act.

The Difference Between Drift Detection and Crisis Detection

Detection at the crisis stage is too late. The curve is already in motion. The damage is already inflicted. Value is already diluted.

Drift detection is the inverse: it catches errors early, when corrective action is still cheap, simple, and reversible.

The reality is stark:

Drift detection is the #1 determinant of whether LP capital remains intact.

GPs who fail to detect drift early are not unlucky. They are unprepared.
